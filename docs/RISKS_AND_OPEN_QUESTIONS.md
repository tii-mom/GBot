# GrowthBot Risks and Open Questions

## 1. Strategic Risks

### 1.1 Too Much Complexity Too Early

GrowthBot includes Bot, Mini App, boxes, points, marketplace, and Agentic Wallet. Building all at once would slow down launch.

Recommendation:

Start with the Telegram growth loop, then add market and on-chain layers.

### 1.2 Weak Launch Urgency

If boxes, rankings, and reports do not create urgency, users may treat GrowthBot as another quest platform.

Recommendation:

Make rank, countdowns, box supply, group competition, and marketplace movement visible from day one.

### 1.3 Mispositioning as a Trading Bot

If users think GrowthBot promises trading profits, expectations and risk increase.

Recommendation:

Position as an auto airdrop and points farming Agent, not a profit-guaranteed trading bot.

## 2. Technical Risks

### 2.1 TON Agentic Wallet Maturity

TON Agentic Wallet is a newer capability and should be treated cautiously until production safety is proven.

Recommendation:

Use it first for low-risk, low-budget, approved actions only.

### 2.2 Wallet Security

Any wallet-related incident can damage trust.

Rules:

- Never custody user main wallet private keys.
- Separate main wallet from Agentic Wallet.
- Hard limits for automated execution.
- User can pause and withdraw.
- Every action is logged.

### 2.3 Randomness Trust

Paid boxes require trustworthy randomness.

Recommendation:

MVP can use transparent server-side randomness. High-value boxes should move toward verifiable randomness.

## 3. Economic Risks

### 3.1 Pay-to-Win Backlash

If paid boxes directly dominate airdrop allocation, free users may reject the product.

Recommendation:

Paid abilities should improve efficiency, but final Claim Credits should include anti-sybil and contribution quality.

### 3.2 Tradable Points Abuse

Fully tradable points can attract farming studios and market manipulation.

Recommendation:

Keep User Score soulbound. Allow trading of abilities and boxes first. Treat Claim Credit trading as a later controlled feature.

### 3.3 Overpromising Airdrops

Promising fixed token conversion creates future liability.

Recommendation:

Use language like "future airdrop eligibility", "allocation weight", "redemption pool", and "project-specific rules".

## 4. Compliance Risks

### 4.1 Blind Box Regulation

Paid randomized boxes with tradable value may raise regulatory questions in some markets.

Recommendation:

- Show rarity tiers.
- Avoid profit language.
- Avoid guaranteed token language.
- Consider market restrictions if needed.

### 4.2 Automated Financial Actions

Automatic swaps or trading can create higher legal risk.

Recommendation:

Do not launch with unrestricted auto-trading. Start with farming, mint, claim, and approved project actions.

## 5. Open Product Questions

Questions to decide:

1. What is the first target region and language?
2. Will paid boxes use TON, Stars, or both at launch?
3. Should marketplace launch before or after TON wallet connection?
4. Are Claim Credits ever tradable, or only redeemable?
5. Will projects be internal only at first, or can external projects apply?
6. What is the first paid product: Agent activation, Alpha Box, or subscription?
7. How aggressive should the referral rewards be?
8. What anti-sybil signals are acceptable before wallet connection?
9. Should there be KOL squad leaderboards in MVP?
10. What is the initial legal jurisdiction and compliance posture?

## 6. Recommended Next Decisions

Before engineering starts, decide:

- MVP user flow.
- First box types and drop contents.
- Point settlement rules.
- Marketplace launch scope.
- TON Agentic Wallet beta policy.
- Revenue priority.
